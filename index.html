I am a Lecturer in Biomathematics at the Department of Mathematics at Imperial College London.

In my research I develop mathematical and computational approaches to understand biological regulation. This includes applying modelling approaches to understand how biological mechanisms work, and developing mathematical and computational approaches to understand how these mechanisms contribute to adaptive function.

Prospective PhD students: If you have a strong quantitative background and are enthusiastic about pursuing an interdisciplinary PhD, you are welcome to go over my research interests and reach out. There is a range of competitive funding schemes available - you should note the specific requirements and deadlines of each scheme.
